Reflection
I've noticed I sometimes struggle to finish side projects, either I lose steam and move on to the next idea, or I overengineer things until I get stuck. This comes from curiosity and a drive to go deep which helps me learn quickly, but I've realised projects don't have to be perfect to be valuable. Lately, I've been setting smaller goals and aiming for a "good enough" version first, which makes it easier to actually ship ideas while still leaving room to improve.
